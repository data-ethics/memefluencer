Why never search on Google?


Google has made such a big name for itself in online search that today we almost only use the term “google” to search online. With an ever-growing number of users and its huge machine learning models, Google is now not only the most used search engine in the world, but also the biggest fish in the sea of personalized ads. On the surface, personalized ads seem great because advertisers are only promoting to their potential customers and users are only seeing relevant ads. If the story ended at this point, there would be no problem. But personalized ads can manipulate users, and even pose a threat to democracy. For instance, manipulation through personalized election ads gained a lot of attention in 2018 with the Cambridge Analytica scandal. At that time, whistleblower Christopher Wylie conducted a revealing interview with the Guardian and the New York Times. If you want to learn more about this scandal, the best way is to watch the Netflix documentary "The great Hack"

With every use, Google stores the online activities of its users and adds them to their “psychological user profile”. This profile is used for personalized search results and personalized advertising, among other things. Due to the profiling and personalized search results, users are only shown information that has been filtered according to their needs; this effect is also known as the "filter bubble". In this way, Google and YouTube search results display only the results that the search engine believes the user wants to see. This restriction of information through algorithmic filtering means that the likelihood of a user finding information that contradicts their profile is low. This also limits the user's ability to learn about other views on the topic. Research has shown that manipulating search engines by displaying search results favorable to a candidate can even influence election results. If this is done with the intention of changing election results in favor of a candidate, it is a highly unethical practice and a danger to democracy. This is exactly what Cambridge Analytics and Trump's election campaign are accused of doing.

How a person's profile can be created and what Google searches reveal about a person's life and psychology is shown in the interactive documentary “Made to Measure” (only in German). Here you can participate interactively in the documentation and also learn something about yourself with a few data that you enter during the observation period.

How to live without Google?

  • Change the default Browser and default search engine:
    • There are some alternatives to Google Chrome and the Google search engine that protect your privacy and don't bother you with ads. For example, you can use DuckduckGo instead of Google Search and set it as your default search engine. A safe browser is Mozilla Firefox, for example. To change the default search engine, follow the steps below:
    • Go to the settings of the browser
    • SSelect Search in the left bar
    • Select Default Search Engine
    • Select DuckDuckGo from the drop-down menu


  • Move your Passwords and Bookmarks to new Browser:
    • If you have been using a certain browser for a long time, switching to a new one can be difficult because you don't want to give up your already saved bookmarks and passwords. Generally, it is advisable to save your username and password on an external drive rather than in the browser, but you can easily transfer your passwords and bookmarks from Chrome to Firefox. When you install Firefox, you get a default option to import your passwords and bookmarks from Chrome. That' s smooth and simple.
  • Disable personalized ads
    • Go to your Google Account.
    • In the navigation pane, select Data and Privacy.
    • Scroll down to “Things you've done and places you've been”.
    • Under “Ads settings”, select Ads personalization.
    • Turn off the switch next to “Ad personalization is turned on”.
